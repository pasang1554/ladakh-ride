How to Hire the Right Cab That Completes Your Tour

By Ladakh Ride Team • 5 min read

Traveling in Ladakh is unlike any other destination in India. The terrain is rugged, the altitude is high, and the roads can be challenging. Choosing the right cab and driver is not just about transportation—it's about safety, comfort, and making the most of your journey.

1. Choose the Right Vehicle for the Terrain

While smaller cars can manage local Leh sightseeing, trips to Pangong Lake, Nubra Valley, or Tso Moriri require robust vehicles with good ground clearance. The Toyota Innova and Mahindra Scorpio are the most popular choices for a reason—they offer the power and stability needed for high mountain passes.

2. Local Drivers are Key

A local driver doesn't just drive; they are your guide, mechanic, and friend. They know the weather patterns, the road conditions, and how to handle acute mountain sickness (AMS) emergencies. Always opt for a registered local taxi union river.

3. Check for Permits and Papers

Ensure your cab has a commercial license plate (yellow plate). Private white-plate vehicles are often stopped at checkpoints, which can cause unnecessary delays in your itinerary.

4. Discuss Your Itinerary

Before finalizing, discuss your full plan with the agency. Some remote routes (like Chusul or Hanle) might require specific permits or additional fuel arrangements.
